Your 4-Year Playbook

What you do in high school shapes what's possible after.
Here's how to play it right — starting now.
9th Build 10th Explore 11th Execute 12th Launch
Academics Your GPA starts now and never resets. First impressions matter — build strong study habits from day one. Add an AP or dual enrollment course if you're ready. Challenge yourself — colleges and programs notice. Take the hardest courses available to you. Academic rigor shows commitment — it opens doors. Stay focused. Colleges review your senior grades even after you're accepted. Don't coast.
Testing Learn what the SAT and ACT are. Understand the format and timeline so nothing surprises you. Take the PSAT in October. See where you stand. Decide: SAT or ACT — which plays to your strengths? Take your SAT or ACT. Aim to finish testing by spring of junior year — before applications begin. Retake if your score can improve. File your FAFSA as soon as it opens (Oct 1) — earlier = more money.
Get Involved Try things. Clubs, sports, volunteering, community. Find what you actually care about — not what looks good. Narrow it down. Commit to 2–3 things you're genuinely invested in. Depth beats breadth every time. Take a leadership role. Captain, president, organizer. Let your story start to take shape. Document everything — hours, roles, achievements. Your activity record tells colleges who you are.
Think Ahead Job shadow. Find someone doing work you're curious about and ask to spend a day with them. Just start looking. Research schools, trades, and programs. No limits yet — explore everything. You don't have to decide yet. Visit campuses, programs, and job sites. Crown Roots can help fund this. See it in person — it changes everything. Make your decision. National Decision Day: May 1. You've earned this moment. Commit and go.

After High School — Your Options

  • 4-Year University Traditional degree. Biggest investment, biggest doors opened. Crown Roots matches you to schools YOU can afford.
  • Community College → Transfer Lower cost. Same destination. A smart, strategic path that works — especially if finances are tight.
  • Trade / Vocational School In-demand skills, strong income, and a faster path into the workforce. Electricians, welders, and HVAC techs earn well.
  • Military Training, education funding, and a career in one. Takes the ASVAB. ROTC can pay for college too.
  • Apprenticeship / Workforce Earn while you learn. Real skills. Real pay from day one. Many trades are actively hiring young people.
